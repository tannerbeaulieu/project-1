About NASA

The National Aeronautics and Space Administration is an independent agency of the United States Federal Government who is responsible for the civilian space program, as well as aeronautics and aerospace research. NASA was established in 1958 with the ultimate goal of having a distinct civilian orientation to encourage peaceful applications in space science. Since its establishment, NASA has successfully led many space exploration missions by conducting the Apollo Moon landing missions, the Skylab space station, and the creation of the Space Shuttle, amongst others. NASA assists in supporting the International Space Station and oversees the development of the Orion Multi-Purpose Crew Vehicle, the Space Launch System and Commercial Crew vehicles.
